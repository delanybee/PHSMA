Pipeline Aging Data — Visualization Options

Data: PHMSA Form 7000-1.1 · 2025 · 223,762 miles reported by installation decade · Hover all charts for details

Plotly.js options
Plotly

Option 1 — Treemap

Area = miles. Largest rectangles are immediately obvious. Colors map to age tier. Annotate with construction era.

Best for: "At a glance, what fraction of the network is old?" The 1950s and 1960s boxes are massive — viscerally larger than the 2020s box. No legend needed. Hover shows miles + context.

Plotly

Option 2 — Sunburst (2-ring)

Inner ring = regulatory era. Outer ring = installation decade. Tells both the big story and the detail simultaneously.

Best for: Showing the hierarchy — pre-EPA → 1950s / 1960s — in one elegant shape. Click any inner segment to zoom in on that era. The "pre-EPA" wedge visually dominates.

Plotly

Option 3 — Waterfall / Cumulative Build

"The network was assembled in waves." Each bar = miles added per decade, cumulating to today's total.

Best for: Showing the construction timeline as a narrative — the post-war pipeline boom of the 1950s–60s, the lull in the 80s–00s, and the shale-era surge of the 2010s. Easy to read as a story of eras.

Plotly

Option 4 — Lollipop Chart (ranked)

Cleaner than a bar chart. Sorted by miles. The 2010s shale-boom decade and 1950s post-war boom dominate instantly.

Best for: Direct comparison of decades ranked by size. The fact that the 2010s (#1) and 1950s (#2) are separated by 60 years is a powerful story beat.

Apache ECharts options
ECharts

Option 5 — Nightingale Rose Chart

Each petal = one decade. Radius = miles. Color gradient from deep red (oldest) to dark green (newest). Very editorial / magazine-style.

Best for: Investigative journalism hero visual. Visually striking — the 1950s and 1960s petals tower over the pre-1920 and 1920s ones. The shale-era 2010s petal shows the modern boom. Immediately shareable.

ECharts

Option 6 — Theme River (Streamgraph) 2017–2025

How has the composition of the network changed year over year? Each band = one installation decade. Width = miles active that year.

Best for: "Is anything changing?" The 2010s band (green) grows — new shale-era pipe being added. But the old red/orange bands stay stubbornly wide, showing the oldest infrastructure is not being retired. The answer is mostly: no.

ECharts

Option 7 — Radial Bar (Polar) Chart

Concentric arcs, one per decade. Length = miles. A non-traditional alternative to a standard horizontal bar.

Best for: A visually interesting sidebar or supporting chart. The 2010s outer ring and 1950s ring competing for dominance reads as "old and new infrastructure neck and neck."

Vanilla JavaScript / Canvas options
Canvas JS

Option 8 — Waffle Chart (Unit Chart)

Each square = 1,000 miles. 224 squares total. Color = installation decade. Count the red squares — that's old infrastructure.

Best for: "Count the red squares" journalism. A general audience immediately understands that each square is a real, physical chunk of pipeline. No axis needed. The red pre-EPA squares overwhelming the canvas is the whole story.

All charts use 2025 PHMSA Form 7000-1.1 data · 223,762 miles (excluding unknown install date) · Built with Plotly.js 2.35 and Apache ECharts 5.5
